About Me

I am currently an ATER at Paris Nanterre University.

Since October 2023, I have also been a postdoctoral researcher at CMAP (École Polytechnique), in the PEIPS team. My research is partially supported by an INSERM/INCa project in collaboration with Saint-Louis Hospital, as well as by the ERC SINGER obtained by Sylvie Méléard. The postdoctoral project focuses on understanding cancer cell invasion through two main aspects: a theoretical part based on the multiscale analysis of a birth-and-death process, and an applied part centered on the analysis of clinical data.



I obtained a joint PhD between the University of Paris-Est Créteil (LAMA) and the University of Sfax, Tunisia, under the supervision of Arnaud Le Ny and Mondher Damak. I defended my thesis in December 2023. My research focused on stochastic differential equations and Hawkes processes applied to long-range neural networks.

Research Topics

  • Interacting particle systems: Hawkes processes, birth-death processes, invasion models, Langevin equations
  • Stochastic analysis and large-scale limits: pseudo-periodic solutions, stochastic differential equations with jumps, long-time behavior of long-range interactions, population dynamics limits
  • Biology and clinical data analysis: dynamic Bayesian networks
